Meditation Definition

Meditation is a practice that involves training your mind to focus and redirect your thoughts, often with the goal of achieving a mentally clear and emotionally calm state. It can involve focusing on an object, sound, breathing, or a mantra. It's used to reduce stress, anxiety, and improve well-being.

Key Aspects of Meditation

Focus

Concentrating on a specific object, sensation, or thought to quiet the mind.

Relaxation

Helps to lower stress and promote calmness.

Mindfulness

Being present in the moment, observing thoughts and feelings without judgment.

Variety of Techniques

Including focused attention, open monitoring, and loving-kindness meditation.

Major Meditation

Focused Attention Meditation trains the mind by concentrating on a single point like breath, mantra, or sensation.

Automatic Self-Transcending is effortless meditation that induces transcendence with minimal cognitive control.

Open Monitoring observes the present moment continuously without judgment, accepting all experiences as they arise.
